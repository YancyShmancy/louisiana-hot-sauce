The Set List

Our Signature

Three typefaces are utilized to represent the three main ingredients that comprise our hot sauce.

  • “the original”- Karmina - represents cayenne peppers
  • “Louisiana”- Bangla mn with stoke - represents vinegar
  • “Hot sauce”- Trebuchet ms with stroke - represents salt and black pepper

Stacked Logo:

  • These typefaces should only be used in the logo.
  • The logo should never be smaller than it appears on the bottle. Measure from the pepper clef to the E in Hot Sauce. (2.1068in x 1.1448in)
  • The logo should always appear stacked.
  • Measure clear vertical space by the height of the pepper clef.
  • Never allow typography or other elements to invade the logo.
  • Never alter the placement and size relationship of its typography or Pepperclef symbol.

Logo Specifications

  • Always use on dark background when type is present.
  • Logo may never be used on background whose color is equal value or brighter than Pantone 486 C.
  • Primary color lockup:
    • Pepperclef body is Pantone 7427 C
    • Clef and “Louisiana” are Pantone 564 C
    • High light on Pepperclef, “the original”, and “hot sauce” are Pantone 486 C.
    • If color cannot be used, everything in logo is black, except the highlight on the Pepperclef is knocked out - If black background, reverse out.
  • Logo can appear in all one color if it is one of the 3 colors in the original logo.
  • Preference for all one color goes to red, but is determined by the value of the background.
  • The flavor should never lock up with the logo and should always be tied to character.
  • The tagline should never lock up with the logo.

The Pepperclef

The logo mark, coined the “Pepperclef,” exemplifies the flavor of jazz with the soul of our hot sauce, by utilizing the body of a cayenne pepper and the stem of a treble clef.

Pepperclef Specifications

  • The Pepperclef should always make the "L" in Louisiana.
  • The Pepperclef may appear without the type, but the type may never appear without the Pepperclef.
  • Pepperclef, when used alone, should be used on light background.
  • Pepperclef, when used alone, should always be one color.
  • Color of Pepperclef may be altered within the established color pallet.

Why we sing, "Heat the Soul"

“Heat the soul” is meant to embody everything we stand for as a brand. We want to combine the flavor of our hot sauce with the soul of jazz culture. The soul in Louisiana’s culture is something to be remembered and appreciated, just like our sauce.

Tag Specifications:

  • The tagline will never be on a straight baseline to ensure the reader feels movement and flow of the brand.
  • Tagline will always be in Futura Medium Italic white.
  • Nothing can overlap the tagline.
  • Tagline should never be less than 7pt.

Meet the Band

Our iconography represents the movement, spirit and essence of our brand. Although they share a rhythmic style, each icon is unique.

Flavor Characters

  • They always appear in white and on a colored background
  • They may be used in the positions as they appear on the bottle, or reflected horizontally when used without type.

The Original Swing

“Swing” represents originality in its truest form. He doesn’t need an instrument to show his rhythm. The fiery background of his label reveals the power within every bottle. Rhythm comes from within the person and “Swing” proves that every time he hits the mic.

Habanero Hank

“Hank” was born to play hot jazz. This style earned its fame by a buildup in the music that led to an energy-packed chorus. The red, fiery background of his label symbolizes the heat delivered in every drop. The saxophone brings a powerful flavor of Habanero, the hottest of our sauces.

Bebop the Jalapeno

“Bebop” is a spicy cat who plays the trumpet better than anyone on the block. His trumpet’s sound is almost as hard and spicy as the famous jalapeno flavor. The green background on his label symbolizes the color’s relation to life and vibes well with a red tone.

Chipotle Chipper

“Chipper” transforms an appreciation for jazz into a jam session. His guitar can be heard playing sweet jazz rock at any time of the night. The mellow flavor of Chipotle is illustrated by the cool and consistent background color of his label.

Ragtime Red

“Red” is in his comfort zone every time he plays his favorite style of jazz, ragtime. The flute plays a happy, energetic sound that is represented by the vibrant blue and yellow background of his label. Red’s chili flavor has the zing that motivates a guy like Red to play a carefree tune.

Improv Pepper

“Improv” is at his best behind his piano with eyes closed and fingers playing. The elegant background of his label represents his sophistication and a refined maturity, while his free flowing style relates to anybody’s ability to amplify a meal with a sauce like the roasted pepper.

Roasted Blue

“Blue” is all about the soul. His upright bass represents a staple in the blues style. The multicolored background of his label portrays a witty sense of wisdom. Meals share the same appreciation for the roasted garlic flavor as songs have for the upright bass.

General Iconography

A glossary of secondary iconography supports the overall aesthetics of our brand.

  • The icons must be shown in white or any color in our color pallette and must be used on a colored background of some sort.
  • Icons should always be used as a supporting element, not a main graphic component.
  • A single icon may never be used on its own.
  • Icons can be scaled, rotated or reflected as the user needs, but should never be skewed or warped.
  • Label Shape: The unconventional shape of the bottle label creates another piece of iconography for our brand by revealing an abstract instrument in the negative space on the back of the bottle.

The Heat Wave

Inspired by jazz instruments and the sounds they produce, this mark serves to direct the flow of the reader throughout the copy. The Heat Wave can only be used around body copy longer than one sentence.

The more simplistic version of The Heat Wave is used to section off the website navigation. This iconography is used exclusively on the website for indication of navigtion only. It may not be scaled or altered in any way.

Typographic Elements

Futura’s easy-to-read typeface represents two qualities we pride ourselves on, authenticity and tradition. A geometric sans-serif, Futura is a revival of previous design, brought into a modern context.

  • Futura is our primary typeface used in all communication materials.
  • Type size, weight and color will be used to establish a clear hierarchy of information.
  • Do not substitute any other typeface. Futura should be used in all printed items being distributed.
  • Preferred Casing: Uppercase and lowercase styling in headlines and call outs support our brand warmth and authenticity.

Typographic Style

  • Variety will be used to show hierarchy amongst type family.
  • Futura Light: If the headline is Futura Regular, all body copy is Futura Light. If the headline is Futura Light, all body copy is Futura Regular.
  • Futura Regular: If the headline is Futura Regular, all body copy is Futura Light. If the headline is Futura Light, all body copy is Futura Regular.
  • Futura Medium Italic: Futura Medium Italic is used for the tagline. It can also be used elsewhere in short phrases or headlines as long as the tag is not present.
  • Typography should always be on a colored background.
